How to play Texas Hold'em Poker

Objective of the Game

The goal of Texas Hold'em is to make the best five-card hand possible using any combination of your two hole cards and the five community cards on the table.

The Basics

Texas Hold'em is typically played with 2 to 10 players. The game consists of four betting rounds: Pre-flop, Flop, Turn, and River. At each round, players have the option to bet, check, raise, or fold.

1. The Deal

Each player is dealt two private cards (also called hole cards). These cards are only visible to the player who receives them.

2. The Betting Rounds

There are four rounds of betting in Texas Hold'em:

  • Pre-flop: After each player has been dealt two hole cards, the first round of betting begins. Players can call, raise, or fold.
  • Flop: The dealer deals three community cards face-up in the center of the table. Another round of betting takes place.
  • Turn: A fourth community card is dealt face-up. A third round of betting follows.
  • River: The fifth and final community card is dealt. The final round of betting takes place.

3. The Showdown

After the final round of betting, any remaining players reveal their hole cards. The player with the best five-card hand wins the pot. If two or more players have the same hand, the pot is split equally between them.

Hand Rankings

The following are the hand rankings from highest to lowest:

  • Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all the same suit.
  • Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  • Full House: Three of a kind and a pair.
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.
  • Straight: Five consecutive cards of any suit.
  • Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  • Two Pair: Two different pairs of cards.
  • One Pair: Two cards of the same rank.
  • High Card: When no hand is made, the highest card wins.

Betting Actions

  • Check: When you don't want to bet, but you want to stay in the game. Only available if no one has bet in the current round.
  • Call: To match the current bet made by another player.
  • Raise: To increase the current bet.
  • Fold: To give up your hand and all your chips in the current round.

Tips for Beginners

  • Pay attention to your position at the table — being in a later position can give you an advantage in decision-making.
  • Start with conservative bets and don't overextend yourself early in the game.
  • Keep track of the community cards and think about how they might help or hurt your hand.
  • Know when to fold — sometimes it's better to cut your losses rather than chase a weak hand.
